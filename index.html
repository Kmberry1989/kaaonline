About the Kokomo Art Association

The Kokomo Art Association was organized in 1926 to interest the community in the Arts. Meetings were first held in members' homes and at the Hotel Frances in downtown Kokomo. In 1981, we achieved not-for-profit status and have since grown to include two locations: the Kokomo Art Center in Highland Park and the Artworks Gallery downtown. Our mission is to promote the arts, support local artists, and provide creative opportunities for the entire community.

Artist Alley Project - 12th Annual Call for Entries

In 2012, the Kokomo Art Association and the Greater Kokomo Downtown Association launched a collaborative effort to revitalize a forgotten alley in downtown Kokomo. With support from local businesses and organizations, this once-dark passage was transformed into Artist Alley—an outdoor public art gallery that officially opened on October 3, 2014.

Artist Alley has since become a vibrant destination, offering a dynamic visual experience for residents and visitors alike. The space has expanded opportunities for regional artists, increased public engagement with visual art, sparked community dialogue, and contributed to Kokomo’s cultural tourism.

Project Scope & Openings

The Artist Alley Steering Committee invites artists to submit proposals for public art installations for a 12-month exhibition beginning September 5, 2025. There is no set theme.

  • Murals: Fifteen (15) 8'x8' wall-mounted panels available (1/4” marine board provided). Smaller dimensions are welcome.
  • Sculptures: Two (2) ground-level sites available. Must be mountable on a base not exceeding 4'x4' and 1' high. Max height: 8 feet. Max weight: 200 pounds.

Sales: All artwork is for sale, with a 30% commission retained.

Submission Deadline: Proposals, applications, and a detailed drawing must be received by 5:00 PM on June 2, 2025. Installation is August 30, 2025.
