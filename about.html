About the Kokomo Art Association

The Kokomo Art Association was organized in 1926 to interest the community in the Arts. Meetings were first held in members' homes and at the Hotel Frances in downtown Kokomo. In 1981, we achieved not-for-profit status and have since grown to include two locations: the Kokomo Art Center in Highland Park and the Artworks Gallery downtown. Our mission is to promote the arts, support local artists, and provide creative opportunities for the entire community.

Artist Alley Project

12th Annual Call for Entries

In 2012, the Kokomo Art Association and the Greater Kokomo Downtown Association launched a collaborative effort to revitalize a forgotten alley in downtown Kokomo into a vibrant, open-air gallery space showcasing local artistic talent. The Artist Alley Project transforms bland service alley walls into exciting canvases for murals and installations, bringing art directly to the public and enhancing the downtown experience. Each year, artists are invited to submit proposals for new artworks to be featured in the alley, contributing to the ever-evolving outdoor exhibition.

Guidelines and Application

We are currently accepting submissions for the 12th Annual Artist Alley Project. Artists are encouraged to propose original, family-friendly artwork suitable for outdoor display. Submissions will be reviewed based on artistic merit, feasibility, and adherence to the project's mission of community beautification through art.

Classes & Workshops

Unlock your creative potential! The Kokomo Art Association offers a variety of classes and workshops throughout the year for all ages and skill levels. From foundational techniques to exploring new mediums, our experienced instructors provide engaging and supportive learning environments. Check out our current offerings:

Watercolor Techniques

with Dixie Ben-net

Explore the transparent and luminous qualities of watercolor. This class is designed for both beginners and those with some prior experience who wish to refine their skills. Learn about washes, layering, wet-on-wet, and dry brush techniques to create beautiful watercolor paintings.

Schedule: Tuesdays, 6:00 PM - 8:00 PM

$120 for 4 weeks

Pastels

with Avon Waters

Dive into the vibrant world of pastels. Learn to create stunning, expressive paintings using soft and hard pastels. We will cover techniques such as blending, layering, underpainting, and creating different textures to capture light and atmosphere, particularly in landscapes.

Schedule: Wednesdays, 1:00 PM - 3:00 PM

$130 for 4 weeks

Photography

with Bob Dawson

Unleash the power of your camera! This class covers the fundamentals of digital photography, including understanding camera settings, mastering composition, utilizing natural and artificial lighting, and basic editing techniques to improve your images. Suitable for beginners with any digital camera.

Schedule: Thursdays, 6:00 PM - 8:00 PM

$150 for 6 weeks

Sip & Paint Parties

with Ramona Daniels

Gather your friends and unleash your inner artist in a fun, relaxed, and social setting! Our Sip & Paint parties are perfect for a night out, a celebration, or just trying something new. No prior painting experience is necessary – Ramona will guide you step-by-step to create your own masterpiece. Bring your favorite beverage and snacks!

Schedule: Fridays, 7:00 PM - 9:00 PM

$35 per session

Zentangle

with Don Wilka

Discover the meditative and relaxing art form of Zentangle®. Create beautiful abstract art by drawing structured patterns in a mindful and intentional way. This class is suitable for everyone, regardless of artistic background, and provides a wonderful way to de-stress and focus.

Schedule: Saturdays, 10:00 AM - 12:00 PM

$40 per session

Junk Journaling

with Vivian Bennett

Transform discarded materials into unique and personal journals! This workshop explores the creative process of using found objects, scraps, ephemera, and recycled materials to construct beautiful and meaningful journals. It's a fantastic way to embrace sustainability and express your creativity.

Schedule: Mondays, 6:00 PM - 8:00 PM

$50 for 2 weeks
